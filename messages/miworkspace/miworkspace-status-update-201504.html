Support for Windows 8.1 on Surface Pro 3 Tablets Available

Support for Microsoft Surface Pro 3 tablets is available to units subscribing to MiWorkspace as of April 13, 2015. The new support option extends MiWorkspace's features to the tablet, including managed software and security updates, easy print setup, and support.

Initially, the MiWorkspace standard software will be available on Surface Pro 3s, including Microsoft Office and other basic productivity applications. Additional software will be evaluated for compatibility upon request.

Units interested in purchasing Surface Pro 3 tablets should follow their usual procurement processes. The tablet, the required ethernet adapter, and USB hub are available as a special order. Interested units currently using Surface Pro 3 tablets should contact the ITS Service Center. We will help your unit decide if moving support for the tablets to MiWorkspace will fit your business needs.

New MiWorkspace Features in Mac System Preferences

In May, we will introduce a new feature for MiWorkspace Macs that will allow faculty and staff to customize when repeat update prompts are received. We are adding the features based on feedback we received.

Faculty and staff will have the option to delay repeat update prompts from Managed Software Center for up to three days, and may choose to be prompted outside of business hours. The new settings will be located in Systems Preferences. Managed Software Center will continue to send a notification when a new update is available.

Looking Ahead at Windows 10

Windows 10 will be the next operating system available for MiWorkspace Windows desktops and laptops. Microsoft expects to release the operating system by the end of 2015. After comprehensive compatibility and user acceptance testing, we expect the operating system will be available in MiWorkspace in 2016. We are excited about exploring many of the features in Windows 10, including an improved user-interface, Windows App Store, and enhanced security.

Provide Feedback on the Updated MiWorkspace SLE

ITS released an updated MiWorkspace Service Level Expectations (SLE) document in January. The newest version incorporates feedback we've heard from campus, and provides more specifics (e.g. about mobile device support). A summary of the changes is also available.

Measuring Service Delivery: MiWorkspace Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

Measuring improvement of MiWorkspace service for our customers is a priority for ITS. The data identifies areas we need to improve or maintain quality.

We chose the following six core aspects to report on in every MiWorkspace Service Update.

Response

Actual - 84%
Goal - 90%

Tickets worked on in the target time after the ticket is reported.

Response targets are:

Critical - 10 minutes
High - 20 minutes
Moderate - 2 hours
Normal - 4 hours

Dispatch

Actual - 84%
Goal - 90%

Tickets assigned to staff outside of the ITS Service Center in the target timeframe.

Dispatch targets are:

Critical - 10 minutes
High - 20 minutes
Moderate - 1 hour
Normal - 1 hour

Resolution

Actual - 86%
Goal - 90%

Tickets resolved in the target timeframe.

Resolution targets are:

Critical - 4 hours
High - 24 hours
Moderate - 5 business days
Normal - 10 business days

Customer Tickets

Incidents - 10.94%
Requests - 10.05%

Portion of MiWorkspace customers who submitted tickets.

Resolution by Tier

Tier 1 - 14%
Tier 2 - 74%
Tier 3 - 12%

Tickets resolved by support tier:

Tier 1 - ITS Service Center
Tier 2 - Neighborhood IT
Tier 3 - Engineering teams and other ITS staff

Incident Duration

Critical - 23 hours
High - 8 hours
Moderate - 1 day
Normal - 2 days

Median length of time to resolve incident tickets.
  • All data derived from December 2014-February 2015.
  • Critical, high, moderate, and normal ticket are defined in the MiWorkspace SLE.
