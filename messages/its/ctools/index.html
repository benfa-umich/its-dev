CTools Contingency Planning for End of Fall Term 2009

This is a request for feedback on which CTools functions are particularly important to you and/or your faculty.

At the start of this term, we met with you to provide an update on the CTools mitigation work that had been accomplished since last Winter Term. Since then, the CTools teams have continued to focus on improving the application’s stability and reliability even under very intensive loads such as that experienced at the end of a term. Based on this work and considerable testing, we have a high degree of confidence in the performance of CTools as usage moves through its peak at the end of the term.

Nevertheless, we are also putting in place more significant contingency planning than has previously been the case so that any possible disruption to CTools will be short and very limited. As requested at our meeting, we are sharing one component of the contingency plan if the system starts to experience problems.

CTools functions are presented in a chart below.

  • Based on considerable faculty and student input over multiple years, we have identified a set of components that we define as mission critical (Priority 1 in Green). These tools have the highest priority and will be continuously maintained.
  • A second set of components (Priority 2 in Yellow) are identified as those that can be disabled or turned off for a brief or limited time and reactivated as soon as the load or other performance problem has been addressed. Priority 2 tools are not rank ordered.

Even temporarily disabling a tool will only be pursued if other elements of the contingency plan have not satisfactorily mitigated a performance issue.

Specifically, it would be very helpful to know whether you and/or your faculty view one or more of the Priority 2 tools as especially important in your use of CTools. Please send us an e-mail identifying the item number and a brief rationale regarding its significance if you or your unit generally feels any of these tools should receive higher priority standing.

We also want to let you know that during the recently concluded administration of midterm online teaching evaluations, we successfully instituted 24/7 CTools monitoring so that any problem or degradation would be very quickly identified and addressed. The same monitoring will be in place in December, when online teaching evaluations are available to students (December 4-15) during the entire period of peak usage.

We would appreciate receiving your feedback by November 23 so that the CTools teams have enough time to consider incorporating it in final contingency planning for the end of Fall Term.

Priority of CTools Functionality

1 = Mission Critical Tools. Essential tools needed to provide students class information and/or continue instruction. These tools have the highest priority to be continuously available.

2 = Lower Priority Tools. These are less used, less important tools that have workarounds and/or produce loads that at higher usage levels could become problematic. These may be turned off for the shortest period deemed necessary if performance problems arise.
