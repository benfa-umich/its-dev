Hello deans, directors, and department heads,

I am pleased to announce that Information and Technology Services (ITS) is working on a priority project to replace the CTools technical infrastructure. This important infrastructure change is necessary to meet the increasing demand for a high-capacity, high-performing, and reliable teaching and learning environment.

To accomplish these changes, however, a service interruption is needed. At this time, we anticipate that CTools will be unavailable for two weekend days (Saturday, June 30, 2012 through Sunday, July 1, 2012). This will allow for complete replacement of the server infrastructure and extensive testing.

As a mission-critical teaching and learning service used by students, faculty, and staff throughout the university, we understand that there is never a good time for CTools to be off-line and unavailable. Based on historical CTools usage data and published 2012 academic calendars, we selected dates that we believe avoid disruption to the vast majority of the University's academic cycles.

If there are critical academic activities during this timeframe that could be affected, please contact me as soon as possible at CTools.2012.Outage@umich.edu.

Please feel free to share this information with faculty, staff, and students in your units. We will announce this more broadly to CTools users in the coming weeks and up through the service interruption.

Thank you for your help and support on this much-needed CTools infrastructure enhancement.

Regards,
John Gohsman
Executive Director, NextGen Michigan Program Office and Teaching and Learning
