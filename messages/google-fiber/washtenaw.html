A2Fiber "Call to Action"

Recent studies* have shown a high speed broadband network will reduce our carbon footprint in virtually every area of society, commerce, education and entertainment.

  • ECommerce
  • Telecommuting
  • Distance Learning
  • In-home virtual medical care
  • Smart appliances and feedback on energy usage

These are all ways a high speed network can reduce greenhouse gas emissions. As a local environmentalist you may know of dozens of ways a broadband network or smart grid could make Ann Arbor cleaner.

A2Fiber is a coalition of local governments, educational institutions, nonprofits and businesses who have joined forces and submitted an application for Google fiber. Google will pick a number of communities in which it will install a broadband network that would stream data at 1 gigabit per second, or about 100 times faster than cable or DSL providers.

As an environmental leader in our community, your support can make a difference. Google has indicated that a major component of the selection process will be the strength of the community support and participation below is a list of action items we would like you to promote. In return, we will publicize your organization on our website.

If you need more information, please contact Lisa Raycraft at raycraft@umich.edu or 734-763-6498.

 

* Sources:
Broadband Services: Economic and Environmental Benefits
