Changes to Planview Time Tracking

Effective July 1, there will be changes to project tracking requirements. If you or any of your team members contribute to a defined project within ITS, you will be required to track that time in Planview. The following have been identified as defined projects for Support Services:

  • BYO
  • ITAM/SWLD Software Self-Service Portal
  • MiWorkspace á la carte service creation and implementations
  • ServiceNow Change Management Strategy & Assessment
  • Automation of Provisioning & Deprovisioning Processes
  • Computer Showcase eCommerce/Point-of-Sale

This time tracking will be used by the Support Services lead team and ITS senior leadership to help keep track of projects across ITS. Operational and production support time tracking will still be up to each manager's discretion. All managers should review Planview timesheets regularly for accuracy.

If you have questions regarding this change, contact your supervisor.

Offboarding Update

An automatic process for offboarding all regular faculty and staff from the MiWorkspace service has been rolled out to ITS Support Services. As of June 19, when Human Resources processes a Support Services departure in Wolverine Access, this triggers an automated process to offboard that individual from the MiWorkspace service.

This automated process will cut down on the manual work done by departments and units during the offboarding process and enable MiWorkspace to remove access more efficiently, as well as ensuring more accurate data on who has access to the service.

Following a successful trial, automation will be turned on for remaining ITS department IDs, hopefully as soon as July 2017.

Video: 8 Wastes of Lean

Mark Graban's blog post, Everyday Examples of the 8 Wastes of Lean, points out that Toyota famously changed automobile manufacturing forever by focusing on continuous improvement, particularly on the never-ending process of waste elimination. Over the years, eight specific types of waste were identified. They are now referred to as the 8 Wastes of Lean:

  • Defects
  • Overproduction
  • Waiting
  • Non-Utilized Talent
  • Transportation
  • Inventory
  • Motion
  • Extra Processing

Although Lean was originally developed in the automotive manufacturing sector, every business experiences waste in one form or another. In fact, if you keep your eyes open, you'll notice waste is all around you.

View a 3-minute animation on the 8 Wastes and examples associated with each one. While watching, think about how these wastes relate to your specific area and what you can do to eliminate them and gain more time to perform value-added tasks.
