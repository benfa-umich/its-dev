Knowledge Management Is Good For All Of Us

Knowledge management is back in focus for Support Services, as we implement Knowledge Centered Support. Rollout will be in waves, starting with a small group who are coaching each other through the new practices. Rollout will grow across Support Services and ITS over the next year. KCS practitioners work with knowledge in an integrated way while solving each incident, with the UFFA approach:

  • Use an existing accurate article, when available
  • Fix a draft, incomplete, or inaccurate article
  • Flag articles that need improvement from others
  • Add articles based on the incident at hand

Wave One has 13 participants representing Service Center, NIT, Tier 3 and Depot. It will run through January 2017.

Wave Two will have 26 participants from the same areas and is expected to start in February.

Self-Service Software Center For MiWorkspace Customers

On Friday, November 18th, the MiWorkspace team released a major service change/advancement with the release of Self-Service Software Center; the MiWorkspace App Store. This release was based on a data model that took 8-12 months of tuning and extensive analysis. This major service change/advancement is just one step of many coming innovations and improvements. It represents a repeatable model which the MiWorkspace team will continue to utilize to make decisions. Because of the hard work and dedication, November 18 is now known as "Self-Service Day."

By designing and implementing distinct, representative, and accurate ticket resolution categories in Service Link, we showed the MiWorkspace service spend nearly 10,000 hours per month navigating software requests and delivery for our customers. Data points convinced leadership a change was needed:

  • 4,500 hours are spent navigating approvals for free and university licensed titles, with 10,000 hours per month spent navigating all software requests.
  • 48 business hours are the average wait time for each request for free and University licensed titles.
  • 63% of the 48 business hours were spent waiting for unit approvers to say "yes" to deploy free and University licensed titles.
  • Of all incidents analyzed, all showed they were approved — Not one was denied.

MiWorkspace released the MiWorkspace App store on the Windows platform to address this problem. The Mac platform already had an App store, but now it offer more titles for self service deployment. This service change has many benefits:

  • Reduced unnecessary overhead for our customers.
  • Bolstered our mission to allow our customers to have ready access to the tools they need to support the mission.
  • Standardized pieces of MiWorkspace environment, while simultaneously enabling customers to customize for their needs.

This is the power of scale, and the power of teams of specialists. More innovations and improvements are coming for MiWorkspace in 2017.

Spotlight on MiWorkspace Quality Assurance Team

Thanks to the QA team, most of the nearly 2,000 changes released each year to MiWorkspace go completely unnoticed by the faculty and staff who use a MiWorkspace device. The QA team plays a critical role in the communication process when the user experience is substantially altered by the change. The team coordinates with other technical teams in ITS and U-M to anticipate changes that may impact the software and hardware supported by MiWorkspace.

Using over 75 computers and printers, the QA team analyzes the change using a set of predefined standards to determine:

  • Compatibility with over 200 unique software titles, including supported browsers and university-wide applications and administrative systems.
  • Compatibility with three Windows operating systems (Windows 7, 8.1, and 10) and four Mac operating systems (Mavericks, Yosemite, El Capitan, and Sierra).
  • Performance using current and previous MiWorkspace standard hardware and recommended printer models.
  • Performance with U-M networks (WiFi) and computing environments.
  • Satisfactory software performance (for example, no prompts to upgrade or purchase another product).

The QA lab is often the last step to releasing a change to production. However, significant software updates are released to the MiWorkspace Beta Testers group first. This group consists of about 300 staff from many units that have volunteered to receive changes early and report issues.
