Support Services News

KCS Program Rollout Continues

Support Services is beginning to manage institutional knowledge using Knowledge-Centered Service (KCS) practices. KCS will help us make sure our knowledge is easily accessible to everyone who needs it and ensure repeatable and accurate solutions for ourselves and our customers. ServiceLink is the tool we use to store and access this knowledge.

We are rolling out the KCS program gradually across Support Services. We have completed Wave 1 with a cross-section of 13 staff from the Service Center, Neighborhood IT, Depot, and Engineering and are closing in on the end of Wave 2, which included an additional 26 staff across Support Services. As training and competency tasks are completed, we will be awarding badges to staff at each level.

Want to know more? Visit the U-M Knowledge-Centered Service site and feel free to email questions to itsknowledgeteam@umich.edu. Stay tuned for more information on KCS in upcoming months!

ITS News

Celebrating Duo's First Year at U-M

The university is celebrating its first year of using Duo Security for two-factor authentication.

We are a mobile university! According to Jessica Rohr's article in ITS Connection Points, "U-M logs 75% of its authentications via the mobile app: the industry average for higher education is 40% (according to our Duo partners). With more than 200,000+ successful authentications each week and 8,970 people now protecting their personal and university data by turning on two-factor for Weblogin, U-M data and systems are better protected."

Team Depot Wins Broomball Tournament

Team Depot triumphed at this year's ITS Annual Picnic, defeating several teams to win the broomball tournament. The team was captained by Jeff Trumitch, with Larry Dorton, Zach Goggins, Kristen Bolger, Mark Pugh, Cameron Shawcross, Sebastian Saladiak, and Don Adams.

Here are the highlights from Don:

"First I want to thank the planning committee—they did a great job this year. Also all the other teams played well and left it all out on the ice.

We attacked each round an enthusiasm unknown to mankind. We focused on teamwork, exploited our opponents' weaknesses, and played tough physical broomball. Larry Dorton was our scoring leader and MVP, notching 5 goals including the championship winner in the shoot-out. Jeff Trumitch demonstrated solid goal-tending, shutting out two teams on our road to the finals.

After many of us suffered a heartbreaking loss last year in the finals of tug-o-war, we would not be denied again. We had a chip on our shoulder and something to prove. The win really bought the Depot together and strengthened the already concrete bond we share as a team. We hope this will be the first of many championships and awards the Depot helps bring home to Support Services in the future."
