Support Services News

Service Excellence: Continuous Improvement Course Launches this Week

The next course of the Service Excellence Program is launching within Support Services! The Service Excellence team has been working with an industrial engineer, Dr. Don Lynch from ISD & College of Engineering, to develop the Service Excellence: Continuous Improvement course.

This instructor-led course will explore ways we can maximize customer value while minimizing waste. One of the unique factors about this course is you'll learn by doing instead of "just listening." Participants in this course will spend half their time learning about Lean methodology and discussing some of the tools we're currently using within Support Services. The other half of the course will engage participants through a hands-on simulation to reinforce Lean concepts.

We would like to thank you, again, for your efforts to continuously develop your skills, which enable us to maximize value for our customers.

If you aren't signed up for this course yet, keep your eyes open for a calendar invitation.

ITS News

ServiceLink May 4 Release Changes

The monthly ServiceLink release will be on Thursday, May 4. Though there are no major features or user interface changes included in this release, there are a couple of changes to note.

With the release on May 4, users will see the following:

  • Assignment Group owners will begin to receive notifications when an Incident SLA has reached 50%, 75%, and 100%. Previously, notifications went out to Assignment Group owners at 50% and 100%.

The May 4 release also includes some MiWorkspace only updates:

  • New form offboarding customers from MiWorkspace (automation coming in the future).
  • Update to the field displayed for "Last Logged in User" for Windows machines.
  • Requiring the 'Stockroom' field have a value if a device has a value of 'In Stock'.
  • Limiting the ability to delete devices from ServiceLink to select Depot staff.
  • New Homepage for Neighborhood IT.

As always, we encourage you to join the U-M ServiceLink Google+ Group to both keep informed, and post questions or comments to the community.
