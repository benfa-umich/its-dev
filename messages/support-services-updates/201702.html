Service Excellence Badging Launches

The Service Excellence Program is rolling out a new enhancement—Badging. Badging is a way to reward, recognize, and display skills, abilities, and knowledge gained through the Service Excellence program. It also reinforces collaboration across teams and units, which is a cornerstone of the FOCUS mindset.

Support Services staff can earn their first badge right now by completing the Customer Experience e-Learning. You will receive a congratulatory email along with a laminated badge template. Printed badges can be hung beneath cubicle nameplates, on your walls, or around your team's common area to showcase Service Excellence accomplishments. The following table outlines the badging criteria and rewards.
Badge Criteria Reward
Recruit: View the Customer Experience e-learning Badging name plate (and a Service Excellence sweatshirt, if not previously received)
Starter: Complete the Customer Experience Self-Assessment (Found on the MBlem Site) and debrief with your manager, during your next one-on-one Service Excellence coffee mug
All-American: Create a workgroup consisting of internal co-workers to improve the customer experience in your area by applying concepts from the Customer Experience e-learning Pizza party/breakfast with other All-American badge holders
Coach: Create a cross-team workgroup (representatives from different units) to implement customer experience improvement and share results with SSLT Showcase your efforts during Support Services Leadership Team (SSLT) meeting, lunch provided

The Service Excellence team created a one-minute video to help you navigate the University of Michigan's digital badging website, MBlem.

Service Center Activity: January 2017

Total Contacts

20,945 total • 997 contacts per weekday

January is a big month due to the start of Winter term. Increases were led by help needed for passwords, accounts, and access, including Duo. Support questions for MiWorkspace also increased.

Up 32% from year-ago, and up 56% from month-ago.

How Customers Contacted Us

Per weekday: 470 calls • 290 email & self-serve • 205 access changes • 32 chats

Long-term ambition is to increase adoption of chat and de-emphasize email; no specific project plans or timeline for that yet.

Promptly Answered Calls

78.8% within 30 seconds

Not good—minimum goal is 80% within 30 seconds and our ideal is much higher. Open positions reduced staffing levels and led to substantial delays during January peak calling times. Contrast with year-ago performance of 89.0%

First Contact Resolution

61%

First Contact Resolution (FCR) is strongly correlated with customer satisfaction. Year-ago FCR was 52%—trending nicely.

Customer Satisfaction

97.5%

Consistent high scores, but only a 3% response rate, so scores are a partial picture. Will be important to grow the response rate over time.

Access Terminations

100% completed within 2 weeks

New goal and processes in place for 2017 to remove administrative access for at least 90% of terminated employees and affiliates within 2 weeks. Previous norm was up to 6 months.
