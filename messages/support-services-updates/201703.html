Support Services News

MPrint Mobile App Released

MiWorkspace Engineers Brendan Dailey and Jim Zajkowski, along with all of MiWorkspace, are celebrating the release of the MPrint Mobile App. The MPrint Mobile App is a product of the Bring Your Own Device (BYOD) project. With it, users can release Follow Me print jobs from their smartphone—detecting nearby printers with Bluetooth or by scanning a label on the printer. The first phase of the release includes equipping the Sites printers in the Angell Hall Fishbowl with Bluetooth beacons. MiWorkspace is hoping to expand to all Sites printers by Fall 2017, and offer this technology to all MiWorkspace customers as soon as the pilot phase concludes this summer.

Service Center Activity: February 2017

Total Contacts

17,174 total • 859 contacts per weekday

Demand for support typically moderates after the start of the semester. Overall volume is down 14% from month-ago; down 2% from year-ago.

How Customers Contacted Us

Per weekday: 398 calls • 247 email & self-serve • 189 access changes • 26 chats

Long-term ambition is to increase adoption of chat and de-emphasize email; no specific project plans or timeline for that yet.

Promptly Answered Calls

88.0% within 30 seconds

Improved vs. January due to reduced volume of overall contacts, and onboarding of 2 contractors to fill open service center slots. This is within the range we like to see regularly. Still trailing the February 2016 score of 93.2%, due to open staff positions, and will likely decrease when call volumes increase.

First Contact Resolution

59%

First Contact Resolution (FCR) is strongly correlated with customer satisfaction. Year-ago FCR was 51%—trending nicely.

Customer Satisfaction

96.7%

Consistent high scores, but only a 3% response rate, so scores are a partial picture. Will be important to grow the response rate over time.

Access Terminations

100% completed within 2 weeks

New goal and processes for 2017 remove administrative access for at least 90% of terminated employees and affiliates within 2 weeks. The improvement is huge over previous norms (up to 6 months). We still have work ahead to make these improvements more robust and sustainable.
