Securely Access University Data from Your Personal Devices

It's Good for the U and It's Good for You

The university recognizes members of the university community can be productive and efficient when accessing or maintaining sensitive institutional data on their personally owned devices. To provide guidance for this use, ITS worked with leaders across the university to develop a new policy: Security of Personally Owned Devices That Access or Maintain Sensitive Institutional Data (SPG 601.33), and to determine what types of sensitive data may be accessed or maintained on personal devices.

What This Means for You

As an ITS staff member, you are permitted to use personally owned devices when working with sensitive university data, as long as you properly secure and manage your devices. This minimizes the risk to university data as well as to personal information.

The policy grants departments the discretion to be more restrictive. ITS will follow the policy with this additional restriction:

You may only access or maintain the following data types on appropriately managed and secured personally owned devices when it is imperative to maintaining ITS service availability and integrity:

  • Protected Health Information
  • Student Loan Information
  • Social Security Numbers

To learn more about the policy and about securing your personally owned device(s), see University Data and Personally Owned Devices.
