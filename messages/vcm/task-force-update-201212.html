Video Content Management Project Update

December 2012

Thanks to those of you who recently joined the VCM project email list. Please feel free to share these communications, and our project website with your interested colleagues. Here are a few updates and accomplishment highlights from the project team this month:

Accomplishment Highlights:

  • The Library and Language Resource Center will develop internal workflows to support best practices in instructional and communications video production. This is a new application of the VCM system for content production in academic units, in addition to the existing use for delivering course content to CTools sites.

  • ISR and the School of Education have uploaded more than 12,000 videos as part of The Gates Foundation "Measures of Effective Teaching (M.E.T.) project. Kaltura is used to stream video content to "taggers," who are using a custom web application and custom (non-Kaltura) player to code the videos for social science and education research.

Project News:

  • The VCM Project team will expand the pilot in 2013
    The project team and Executive Steering Committee selected several new video usage scenarios to explore in the early part of winter term. Their efforts should result in a robust video content management service that will meet and support a wide variety of needs. Read More

  • Determining the funding model for the VCM service
    The project team is trying to develop a pricing structure for the VCM service that is both equitable and sustainable. There are a number of factors to consider, including accurately capturing bandwidth usage and the level/quality of customer service support required over the long term. Read More
